Lissajous curves are a way to visualize stereo sound. The left speaker position is plotted on the horizontal axis, and the right speaker position is plotted on the vertical axis. For simple waveforms, this creates recognizable and often beautiful patterns. Matt and I made a simple app to play with these, using sound, drawings, and interactive controls (Chrome only).

Our goal was to help illiterate Moroccans use mobile phones independently. As project manager on a team of Olin design and engineering students, Babson business students, and Moroccan École Nationale de L'Industrie Minérale engineering students, I led the design process through user research, problem definition, prototype iteration, and pilot testing.

User Research

We intervied numerous illiterate working Moroccan adults to understand how mobile phones fit into their lifestyles, including

  • bicycle mechanics
  • produce vendors
  • bakers
  • maids
  • taxi cab drivers
  • construction workers

Most own at least one mobile phone. Some need to use them for work, and all use them to call family and friends.

Problem Definition

Illiterate users struggle to

  • unlock their phone
  • find the contact's number
  • dial the number
  • add contacts
  • add minutes

We focused on finding and dialing contacts because these needs are most time sensitive.

Current Technology

Third hand Nokia bar phones are most common. Contacts are recorded on paper notebooks or scraps of cardboard.

Solution Prototype

We prototyped and pilot tested a transitional solution which accommodated current usage patterns and price constraints while augmenting the memory and learning abilities of illiterate users.

  • Culturally relevant symbols and colored pages aid finding contact
  • Consistent colors and Arabic numerals between notebook and phone assist dialing
  • White cover to blend with Friday traditional garb
  • New literate contacts can write themselves in

As a software engineer for The Echo Nest, one of the projects I worked on was developing internal tools which were used for quality assurance. Data accuracy is of utmost importance to The Echo Nest because data is its primary product, and the contually growing database regularly rolls out to enterprise customers. The tools enabled anyone to view and edit dynamically structured data objects and helped create tasks and workflow for quality assurance interns.
